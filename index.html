Zhe-chen Guo

Hello! I am a PhD candidate in Linguistics at the University of Texas at Austin and member of UT Sound Lab. My research focuses on exploring how listeners' segmentation of the continous speech stream is modulated by intelligibility-enhancing clear speaking styles and how it is guided by various prosodic cues.

In addition to speech perception, I have been working on large-scale acoustic analyses of coarticulation in speech produced under different communicative contexts. Recently, I am also interested in building deep learning models to reconstruct speech features from neural signals.
